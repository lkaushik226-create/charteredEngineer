Key Government Notifications

Know Your Rights & Requirements

Important government notifications and policies that make a Chartered Engineer certificate mandatory or beneficial for your business.

MOEF · 20.01.2016

No NOC for Repaired Electrical/Electronic Assemblies

No NOC is required for repaired electrical/electronic assemblies exported for repair and re-imported. However, a CE certificate is essential before export to satisfy Customs that the assembly was sent for repair and not sold abroad.

Ref: MOEF Notification dated 20.01.2016
Customs · Notif. 91/2009

Installation Certificate for Capital Goods (EPCG / SFIS)

Customs Notification 91/2009 requires an installation certificate from a jurisdictional Central Excise officer OR an independent Chartered Engineer confirming installation and use of imported capital goods within 6 months of import.

Ref: Gazette of India, Part II, Section 3, Sub-section (i)
Companies Act · April 2015

Useful Life of Assets — Depreciation Norms

Ministry of Corporate Affairs amended depreciation norms emphasising Useful Life of assets. Where companies use values different from Schedule II, variations must be certified by an independent technical expert.

Ref: MCA Companies Act Amendment 2013 — Schedule II
NMFP Scheme

Cold Chain Infrastructure — Grant-in-Aid up to 75%

Government of India NMFP scheme provides integrated cold chain and preservation infrastructure from farm gate to consumer. Dairy, meat, aquaculture, and marine product units are eligible. CE Mech. certificate mandatory for all three grant instalments.

Ref: Guidelines for Cold Chain NMFP
Rajasthan Govt. · 2019

Agro-Processing, Agri-Business & Agri-Export Policy 2019

Certificate required from a Mechanical Engineer for Capital Investment Subsidy under the Rajasthan Agro-Processing, Agri-Business and Agri-Export Promotion Policy 2019.

Ref: Rajasthan Agro Policy 2019 — Capital Investment Subsidy
DGFT Scheme

Status Holder Incentive Scheme (SHIS)

Declaration certified by Chartered Engineer for all export items under the Engineering sector as per Column 6(iv) of ANF 3E — stating FOB value of engineering sector exports (excluding iron, steel, automobiles, nuclear reactors, ships).

Ref: DGFT ANF 3E — Status Holder Incentive Scheme
